FIDE Initial Rating Calculator

Your First FIDE Rating: Not yet available (need 5+ real games)

To calculate the initial rating, FIDE starts with 2 draws against fictional 1800-rated opponents — these are already included in the calculation automatically.

Then 5 or more results against FIDE-rated opponents are needed to get your first official rating.

  • If you score 0 points in your first tournament, those results are disregarded.
  • Only enter results from tournaments where you scored at least 0.5 points.
  • If your FIDE rating falls below 1400, you need to start again (minimum 5 games, ≥0.5 pts).
  • Results from tournaments within 26 consecutive months are pooled.

FIDE §7.1.4 — A rating shall be published when based on ≥5 games against rated opponents. Rating must be at least 1400.

FIDE §8.2.1 — If an unrated player scores zero in their first event, that score is disregarded.

Find older games at ratings.fide.com → search your name → Calculations → View each month.

FIDE ratings are published on the 1st of each month. Official calculator: FIDE Rating Change Calculator.

K-Factor rules (March 2024): K=40 for new players (first 30 games) and players under 18 below 2300 · K=20 below 2400 · K=10 at 2400+
